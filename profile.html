Ricardo Ruiz Bogotá · Colombia

Ricardo Ruiz is a political scientist from the National University of Colombia and holds a Master's degree in Public Policy from Universidad de los Andes, with five years of professional experience in data analysis applied to political, institutional, and electoral processes.

His work focuses on the use of quantitative methodologies to understand complex political phenomena and translate them into strategic inputs for decision-making. Throughout his career he has developed research and analysis on electoral behavior, a topic on which he has also published academic articles.

01 Career

He has worked at nationally recognized polling firms, as well as in public and private sector entities, participating in projects related to electoral analysis, institutional diagnostics, and strategic information assessment. His experience combines applied research, large-scale database processing, and the development of analytical tools that illuminate political, territorial, and social dynamics across different levels of government and organizations.

02 Methodology

On the methodological side, his work draws on advanced quantitative techniques, including statistical regressions, classification models, and machine learning methods such as Random Forest, among other data science approaches. These tools enable the identification of patterns, segmentation of complex information, and construction of analytical models that provide solid evidence for strategy design and the interpretation of political scenarios.

03 Approach

Yet his approach extends beyond technical analysis. As a political scientist, his work seeks to connect quantitative results with qualitative interpretations of power, political behavior, and institutions — allowing data to be translated into real strategic decisions. This combination of statistical analysis and political contextual reading is central to his professional approach, especially in projects related to electoral processes, governance, and public decision-making.

04 Team

In many projects, this work is developed in collaboration with an interdisciplinary team, particularly in the design of positioning and decision-making strategies. The combination of data analysis, political contextual reading, and expertise in strategic communication allows quantitative evidence to be translated into concrete actions. In this process, technical analysis is articulated with the experience of strategists with extensive backgrounds in political campaigns and organizational positioning, strengthening the capacity to design robust strategies for both public and private actors.

05 International

Beyond his experience in Colombia, he has participated in advisory and electoral analysis processes in countries such as Mexico, Peru, and Paraguay, which has allowed him to work with different political systems, electoral dynamics, and institutional contexts. If you would like to learn more about his work or explore possible collaborations, feel free to get in touch — he would be happy to discuss projects where data analysis, artificial intelligence, and political insight can generate value for decision-making.
